AI in Finance

AI transforming banking, payments, trading and financial services

01

How AI Is Rewriting Credit Decisioning in Real Time

AI is dismantling decades-old static scorecards and rigid rule-based credit decisioning, replacing them with real-time risk and intent evaluation across digital channels. As transactions accelerate, issuers face mounting pressure to assess creditworthiness instantly and contextually. This shift represents one of the most significant structural changes in consumer and commercial lending in a generation.

02

In Payments, the AI Race Is Also a Governance Test

AI is rapidly embedding itself across the payments stack — spanning fraud detection, compliance, risk management, and customer personalization — but governance frameworks are struggling to keep pace. Industry leaders are grappling with the critical question of who is accountable for AI-driven decisions and how oversight structures should be built. The governance gap may prove as consequential as the technology itself.

03

Acquirers Say Risk and Readiness Are Slowing Agentic Commerce

A new industry study finds that while most acquirers believe their payments infrastructure can support AI-powered agentic commerce, risk management concerns and merchant unpreparedness are acting as significant brakes on adoption. The disconnect between acquirer confidence and merchant readiness is creating friction in the shift toward agent-led transactions. Risk frameworks built for human-initiated payments may not be adequate for the agentic era.

04

Anthropic Turns Claude Into a Front Door for Daily Apps

Anthropic has launched consumer app connectors for Claude, enabling direct integration with financial services platforms including Intuit TurboTax and Intuit Credit Karma alongside a range of commerce and lifestyle apps. The move positions Claude as a unified AI interface for daily consumer activity, including personal finance management. For fintech and financial services firms, the integration signals a new competitive dynamic in AI-powered consumer engagement.

05

How AI Killed Information Asymmetry in B2B Procurement

AI is fundamentally restructuring B2B procurement by eliminating the information asymmetries that once defined supplier-buyer relationships, with direct implications for B2B payments and financial services. Procurement leaders are now equipped with AI tools that compress research, comparison, and vendor evaluation cycles dramatically. The shift is accelerating payment term negotiations and reshaping the economics of B2B financial products like supply chain finance and trade credit.
