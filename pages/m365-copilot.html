Updates, features and news from Microsoft 365 Copilot

#1

Copilot's agentic capabilities in Word, Excel, and PowerPoint are generally available

Microsoft 365 Blog Thu, 23 Apr 2026

Microsoft has officially launched Copilot's agentic capabilities in Word, Excel, and PowerPoint into general availability, representing a major step forward in AI-powered workplace productivity. The rollout means millions of enterprise users now have access to a Copilot that can move beyond single-turn responses to take sustained, multi-step actions within documents and spreadsheets. In Word, Copilot can assist from first draft through final polish, iterating on content based on user direction. In Excel, agentic features enable Copilot to perform complex data analysis, generate formulas, and structure datasets with minimal manual input. PowerPoint benefits from Copilot's ability to build and refine presentation narratives, pulling in relevant content and formatting slides intelligently. Throughout all three applications, the human remains in control, with Copilot acting on explicit delegation rather than autonomously making unreversible changes. This general availability follows an extended preview period during which Microsoft gathered enterprise feedback to refine the experience. The launch is consistent with Microsoft's broader Wave 3 strategy, which emphasizes embedding agentic AI deeply into everyday workflows. IT administrators can manage these features through existing Microsoft 365 admin controls and compliance frameworks. The release signals Microsoft's commitment to making AI not just assistive but genuinely action-oriented within its flagship productivity suite. Organizations that have already adopted Microsoft 365 Copilot licenses will gain access to these capabilities as part of their existing subscription.

Impact Takeaways

  • Word, Excel, and PowerPoint now support multi-step agentic AI actions, reducing manual effort for drafting, analysis, and presentation building.
  • The GA release means enterprise customers on existing Copilot licenses can immediately begin leveraging these capabilities without additional cost.
  • Human oversight remains central — Copilot acts on delegation, preserving user control and auditability for compliance-sensitive organizations.
  • IT teams can govern agentic features through existing Microsoft 365 admin and compliance tooling.
#2

Copilot Cowork: From conversation to action across skills, integrations, and devices

Microsoft 365 Blog Tue, 05 May 2026

Microsoft announced a significant expansion of Copilot Cowork, its agentic execution layer built into Microsoft 365, adding new skills, integrations, and cross-device functionality. Cowork was originally introduced as a way for users to delegate complex, multi-step tasks to Copilot without needing to monitor every action manually. The May 2026 update broadens the scope of what Cowork can accomplish by connecting it to a wider ecosystem of apps and services users rely on daily. New skills allow Cowork to take contextually relevant actions inside Microsoft 365 apps as well as external platforms, bridging the gap between conversation and outcome. Cross-device support means work initiated on a desktop can continue seamlessly on mobile, ensuring continuity for professionals who move between environments. Third-party integrations expand Cowork's reach beyond the Microsoft ecosystem, enabling it to interact with business-critical tools through agentic actions rather than just providing information. The update builds on the Frontier program preview that launched in late March 2026, incorporating real-world user feedback to improve reliability and task completion rates. Microsoft emphasized that users remain informed and in control throughout Cowork's operations, with transparent reporting on actions taken. The expansion is part of Microsoft's broader strategy to make Copilot the central coordination layer for enterprise work, not just a conversational assistant. Organizations already enrolled in Copilot Cowork through Frontier or general access programs will receive the new capabilities as a rolling update.

Impact Takeaways

  • Cowork's expanded skills and integrations enable professionals to delegate end-to-end tasks across both Microsoft and third-party apps from a single interface.
  • Cross-device continuity ensures complex, long-running tasks initiated on desktop are accessible and actionable on mobile devices.
  • The update reduces the need for manual app-switching, consolidating task execution within the Microsoft 365 Copilot conversation experience.
  • Enterprises should assess their third-party app portfolios to identify integration opportunities that can be automated through Cowork.

Microsoft emphasizes that Copilot is built to keep humans informed and in control even as agents become more autonomous. The article serves as both a vision statement and a call to action for enterprise leaders evaluating their AI adoption strategies. It reinforces Microsoft's competitive positioning against rival productivity AI platforms by centering Copilot's design philosophy on empowerment rather than displacement.

Impact Takeaways

#4

Microsoft Agent 365, now generally available, expands capabilities and integrations

Microsoft 365 Blog Fri, 01 May 2026

Microsoft announced the general availability of Agent 365, a security and governance platform designed to give IT and security teams full visibility and control over AI agents operating within their Microsoft 365 environment. The platform was developed in response to the rapid proliferation of AI agents across enterprises, a trend that has introduced new governance challenges as organizations struggle to track what agents are doing and on whose behalf. Agent 365 functions as a centralized control plane, enabling administrators to observe agent activity, enforce policies, and secure interactions between agents and sensitive business data. The GA release includes expanded capabilities beyond the preview, with new integrations that connect Agent 365 to a wider range of Microsoft and third-party agent deployments. Security teams can use the platform to detect anomalous agent behavior, enforce least-privilege principles, and generate audit logs suitable for compliance reporting. The tool is directly integrated with Microsoft's existing security ecosystem, including Defender and Purview, allowing organizations to apply familiar governance frameworks to AI agent workloads. Microsoft positions Agent 365 as an essential component of responsible agentic AI deployment, particularly for regulated industries. The platform supports both agents built in Copilot Studio and those deployed through other Microsoft 365 channels. With agentic capabilities now generally available across Word, Excel, PowerPoint, and Cowork, Agent 365 provides the governance layer needed to scale these deployments safely. Pricing and licensing details are tied to existing Microsoft 365 and security subscription tiers.

Impact Takeaways

  • Agent 365 is now GA and provides the centralized governance layer enterprises need to safely scale AI agent deployments within Microsoft 365.
  • Integration with Microsoft Defender and Purview means security teams can apply existing compliance workflows to AI agent activity.
  • The platform directly addresses agent sprawl, a growing risk as organizations deploy multiple autonomous agents across business functions.
  • Regulated industries should prioritize Agent 365 deployment alongside any rollout of agentic Copilot features to maintain audit trail integrity.
#5

New and improved: Agent governance, intelligent workflows, and connected app experiences

Microsoft 365 Blog Mon, 11 May 2026

Microsoft released its April/May 2026 Copilot Studio update, delivering a set of improvements focused on agent governance, workflow intelligence, and app connectivity for enterprise developers and administrators. The update strengthens governance capabilities, giving IT teams more granular control over how agents operate, what data they can access, and how they interact with users and systems. Intelligent workflow enhancements allow agents built in Copilot Studio to handle more complex, conditional logic without requiring custom code, lowering the barrier for business users to create sophisticated automations. Connected app experiences have been expanded, enabling agents to interact with a broader range of business applications directly from within the Copilot Studio environment. One of the headline additions is an expanded agent usage estimator, which provides organizations with better tools to forecast how many agent actions their workflows will consume — a critical planning capability as usage-based billing becomes more common. The update also includes improvements to the Prompt Editor, building on changes introduced in the April release to accelerate iteration cycles for agent builders. Multi-agent orchestration capabilities, which became generally available in a prior update, receive further refinements to improve reliability in complex agent pipelines. These updates collectively make Copilot Studio a more robust platform for enterprises looking to build, deploy, and govern custom agents at scale. Microsoft continues to invest in Copilot Studio as the primary no-code/low-code development environment for Microsoft 365 Copilot extensibility. Organizations actively building agents should review the new governance controls and usage estimator features to optimize both security posture and licensing costs.

Impact Takeaways

  • The expanded agent usage estimator helps organizations accurately forecast and manage Copilot consumption costs before scaling agent deployments.
  • Enhanced governance controls give IT administrators more precise oversight of agent permissions and data access, reducing security risk.
  • Intelligent workflow improvements allow non-developers to build more sophisticated agent automations without writing custom code.
  • Enterprises building on Copilot Studio should review the new connected app integrations to identify opportunities to eliminate manual handoffs between systems.
