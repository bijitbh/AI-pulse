Updates, features and news from Microsoft 365 Copilot

#1

Copilot's agentic capabilities in Word, Excel, and PowerPoint are generally available

Microsoft 365 Blog Thu, 23 Apr 2026

Microsoft has officially launched Copilot's agentic capabilities as generally available in Word, Excel, and PowerPoint, marking a significant milestone in the evolution of AI-assisted productivity. The update moves Copilot beyond simple suggestion and generation tasks into active, multi-step execution of complex workflows within each application. In Word, Copilot can now take documents from first draft through to final polish, handling research, restructuring, and revision autonomously. In Excel, the agentic model can execute multi-stage data analysis, apply formulas, and surface insights without requiring step-by-step user prompting. PowerPoint users can now delegate end-to-end presentation creation, with Copilot managing layout, content sourcing, and design coherence. The GA release signals Microsoft's commitment to embedding true agentic AI — not just assistive AI — into everyday office tools used by hundreds of millions of professionals. Users retain full control and oversight at every stage, with the ability to review, redirect, or override Copilot's actions. The launch is part of Microsoft's broader Wave 3 strategy for Microsoft 365 Copilot, which emphasizes moving from reactive assistance to proactive execution. Enterprise organizations can now deploy these capabilities at scale across their Microsoft 365 environments without early-access restrictions. This release is expected to substantially accelerate productivity gains for knowledge workers who rely heavily on document creation and data analysis workflows.

Impact Takeaways

  • Agentic Copilot in Word, Excel, and PowerPoint is now GA — organizations can deploy at scale immediately without early-access enrollment.
  • Professionals can delegate complex, multi-step document, data, and presentation tasks to Copilot, reducing manual effort significantly.
  • Users retain full control and oversight throughout agentic workflows, addressing enterprise concerns about AI autonomy.
  • This GA launch represents a shift from AI as a suggestion engine to AI as an active execution layer within core Office apps.
#2

Bring your everyday business apps into the flow of work with agents in Microsoft 365 Copilot

Microsoft 365 Blog Mon, 13 Apr 2026

Microsoft has announced a major expansion of Microsoft 365 Copilot that brings everyday business applications directly into the AI-powered flow of work. Rather than requiring users to switch between Copilot and external tools, agents can now surface and interact with go-to business apps inside the Copilot conversation interface. This capability closes the long-standing gap between AI-powered insight and tangible in-app action, enabling users to complete tasks in third-party systems without context-switching. The integration leverages the agent framework built into Microsoft 365 Copilot, allowing organizations to connect CRM platforms, ERP systems, HR tools, and other line-of-business applications. Actions taken through Copilot agents can trigger real operations within connected apps, such as updating records, submitting approvals, or retrieving live data. This development is particularly impactful for enterprise users who operate across many tools daily, as it positions Copilot as a unified action layer across the business software ecosystem. The feature aligns with Microsoft's broader strategy of making Copilot an orchestration hub rather than a standalone assistant. IT administrators and developers can configure which apps are accessible through Copilot agents using governance controls in Copilot Studio. The announcement reinforces Microsoft's competitive positioning against other workplace AI platforms that are racing to deliver cross-application agent capabilities. For organizations already invested in Microsoft 365, this update substantially increases the ROI of their Copilot licenses by extending AI-driven automation across their entire software stack.

Impact Takeaways

  • Copilot agents can now trigger real actions in connected third-party business apps, eliminating the need to switch between tools.
  • Enterprise teams can centralize workflows across CRM, ERP, HR, and other systems through a single Copilot conversation interface.
  • IT admins can govern which apps are exposed to Copilot agents via Copilot Studio, maintaining security and compliance controls.
  • This update significantly expands Copilot's value proposition for organizations with complex, multi-app workflows.
#3

Copilot Cowork: Now available in Frontier

Microsoft 365 Blog Mon, 30 Mar 2026

Microsoft has opened up Copilot Cowork to participants in the Frontier program, its early-access initiative for cutting-edge Microsoft 365 Copilot capabilities. Copilot Cowork is specifically designed for long-running, multi-step work scenarios that go beyond quick prompts or single-task completions. The feature allows professionals to delegate sustained, complex workflows to Copilot, which then coordinates across Microsoft 365 apps and connected services to drive work to completion. Frontier participants can now explore Cowork's ability to manage sequential and parallel task execution, handling workflows that may span hours or even days. The system is built to keep humans informed and in control at key decision points throughout the process, distinguishing it from fully autonomous AI systems. Microsoft describes Cowork as the execution layer for Microsoft 365, sitting above individual app-level features and enabling enterprise-grade task delegation at scale. The Frontier program availability signals that Cowork is moving toward broader general availability following validation and feedback from early adopters. Organizations in regulated industries or with complex operational workflows stand to benefit most from Cowork's ability to orchestrate sustained AI-driven work. The launch builds on the foundational announcement of Copilot Cowork made earlier in March and adds program access details for enterprises eager to pilot the technology. Microsoft's phased rollout approach through Frontier reflects its strategy of maturing agentic features responsibly before broad deployment.

Impact Takeaways

  • Frontier program members can now pilot Copilot Cowork for long-running, multi-step task delegation in Microsoft 365.
  • Cowork positions Copilot as an execution layer capable of sustaining complex workflows across hours or days, not just single interactions.
  • Human oversight is preserved at key decision points, making the feature suitable for enterprise and regulated-industry use cases.
  • Early access through Frontier allows organizations to validate Cowork's capabilities and provide feedback ahead of general availability.

New and improved: Multi-agent orchestration, connected experiences, and faster prompt iteration

Microsoft 365 Blog Wed, 01 Apr 2026

Microsoft has announced a wave of updates to Copilot Studio, with multi-agent orchestration systems now reaching general availability. This milestone means organizations can deploy networks of specialized AI agents that collaborate to handle complex, distributed workflows within the Microsoft 365 ecosystem. The GA release of multi-agent systems enables enterprises to build production-grade agentic pipelines where individual agents handle specific domains while an orchestrator coordinates the overall workflow. Alongside the GA announcement, Microsoft has released a significantly improved Prompt Editor that allows developers and power users to iterate on agent prompts faster and more effectively. The updated Prompt Editor includes tools for real-time testing, comparison, and refinement of prompt configurations, reducing the time needed to tune agent behavior for specific business use cases. Governance controls have also been strengthened, giving IT administrators better tools to monitor, audit, and manage agent activity at scale across the organization. Connected experiences improvements allow agents built in Copilot Studio to more seamlessly interact with Microsoft 365 apps and external data sources. These updates reflect Microsoft's commitment to making Copilot Studio not just an agent builder but a full-lifecycle management platform for enterprise AI systems. The combined GA of multi-agent orchestration and improved tooling lowers the barrier for organizations to move from single-agent pilots to full-scale, multi-agent deployments. For professionals building AI solutions on the Microsoft platform, these updates represent a substantial increase in the sophistication and manageability of what can be created.

Impact Takeaways

  • Multi-agent orchestration systems in Copilot Studio are now GA, enabling production-grade deployment of collaborative AI agent networks.
  • An improved Prompt Editor accelerates iteration and tuning of agent behavior, reducing development time for enterprise AI solutions.
  • Strengthened governance controls give IT teams better visibility and management capabilities over agent activity across Microsoft 365.
  • Organizations can now confidently scale from single-agent pilots to complex, multi-agent workflow deployments on the Microsoft platform.
#5

Powering Frontier Transformation with Copilot and agents

Microsoft 365 Blog Mon, 09 Mar 2026

Microsoft has officially introduced Wave 3 of Microsoft 365 Copilot, framing it as a transformative new version of the platform that fundamentally changes the role AI plays in enterprise work. Unlike previous waves focused on assistance and suggestion, Wave 3 centers on embedded agentic capabilities that allow Copilot to act independently on complex tasks within and across Microsoft 365 applications. The announcement marks a strategic inflection point for Microsoft, positioning AI as no longer a supplemental tool but an integrated operational layer within the Microsoft 365 suite. Wave 3 introduces new agent types with specialized capabilities, as well as deeper integration between Copilot and core Microsoft 365 workloads including Teams, Outlook, Word, Excel, and PowerPoint. Microsoft's Frontier transformation concept suggests that the most forward-thinking organizations will use Wave 3 capabilities to fundamentally redesign how work gets done, not just automate existing processes. The embedded agentic model means Copilot can proactively initiate actions, track progress, and adapt to changing circumstances without requiring constant user direction. Enterprise leaders are encouraged to develop Frontier strategies that align AI agent deployment with their specific business transformation goals. The Wave 3 announcement also sets the foundation for features like Copilot Cowork and the agentic capabilities in core Office apps that followed in subsequent weeks. Microsoft is positioning Wave 3 as a competitive differentiator for organizations looking to achieve meaningful productivity and operational gains from AI investment. For IT decision-makers and business leaders, Wave 3 represents the clearest articulation yet of Microsoft's long-term vision for AI in the enterprise.

Impact Takeaways

  • Wave 3 redefines Microsoft 365 Copilot as an embedded agentic platform, not just an AI assistant — a foundational shift for enterprise AI strategy.
  • Organizations should begin developing Frontier transformation strategies that leverage agentic Copilot capabilities to redesign core workflows.
  • Wave 3 provides the strategic framework underpinning all subsequent Copilot feature releases, including Cowork and agentic Office app capabilities.
  • IT and business leaders need to reassess Copilot deployment plans in light of Wave 3's expanded agentic scope and cross-application integration.
